## 4. Bottom-Line Strategy Notes

1. **Medicare connectors split into TWO Palmetto stacks, not three.** NC + SC share Palmetto JM. GA uses Palmetto JJ. Build one JM connector and one JJ connector; share the underlying Palmetto auth/EDI library.
2. **CGS JC (DME) is the single biggest reuse win** — one connector serves all three pilot states (and 14 other states for future rollout).
3. **MMIS X12 stacks are surprisingly reusable.** All three have Gainwell DNA in their fiscal-agent lineage (NC: post-CSRA/CSC; SC: BCBS-SC + historic Gainwell `[Confirm]`; GA: Gainwell since 2010). Trading-partner code, SFTP/PGP envelopes, 837/270/278/835 profiles are 70–80% portable.
4. **The HIE layer is the LEAST reusable.** Three different vendors (SAS, `[Confirm SCeHA vendor]`, Velatura), three different platform APIs, three different participation models (mandatory-but-suspended vs voluntary vs voluntary). Build `hie-service` with a strategy-pattern adapter layer.
5. **GA's CMO transition is the highest-risk pilot exposure.** The 2025 procurement outcome was contested through May 2025 and the operational lineup may shift mid-pilot. Use `state-config-service` versioning and feature flags so MedGuard360 can switch between incumbent (3-plan) and new-award (4-plan) configurations atomically.
6. **SC's nursing-facility MCO carve-in (1/1/2026) is the biggest claim-routing change** of any pilot state — what was FFS NF billing is now MCO encounter. Update claim-routing logic accordingly.
7. **GA Pathways to Coverage is the only pilot-state program requiring a work-activity reporting connector** — unique to GA's `state-config-service` package; no NC/SC analog.
8. **Expansion FMAP only applies to NC.** Pricing models for federally-matched MedGuard360 functions should reflect that GA Pathways services and all SC services get traditional FMAP (~65–70%), not the 90% expansion match.
9. **MFCU recovery profile differs dramatically.** NC and GA are top-tier (8th in NC; top-4 nationally in GA for FY2025 civil recoveries). SC's smaller MFCU operates at proportional scale. The MedGuard360 fraud-engine handoff SLA should match each state's enforcement appetite — particularly tight in GA.
10. **(Phase 2) Palmetto MAC stack consolidates further.** NC/SC/VA/WV all run **JM** — one Palmetto-JM connector serves four pilots. GA/TN run **JJ** — one Palmetto-JJ connector serves two. The CGS-JC DME connector continues to cover all six. Total Medicare-rail connector inventory across the six-state Phase 1+2 footprint: **3 connectors** (JM, JJ, JC).
11. **(Phase 2) Gainwell MMIS pattern reuse is now the dominant economy.** NC (Gainwell), GA (Gainwell), WV (Gainwell), and TN (Gainwell-claims-module) all share Gainwell EDI/trading-partner conventions. VA is partial (Gainwell PRSS only; core MMIS remains Conduent). SC is `[Confirm]`. The MedGuard360 Gainwell-pattern X12 adapter is reusable across 4–5 of 6 pilots.
12. **(Phase 2) TN is architecturally unique.** Managed-care-only since 1994 means **no FFS rail to integrate** — skip the FFS claims connector entirely, focus on encounter ingestion + capitation reconciliation. **Dual-track Program Integrity** (TennCare OPI for provider, TennCare OIG for recipient) requires a fraud-alert router with two endpoints. **Shared Savings reporting** under TennCare III creates a unique financial-reporting hook with no analog in other pilots.
13. **(Phase 2) VA is the highest-revenue-density pilot.** With ~2M enrollees and a low traditional FMAP (~51%), VA's state-share burden is among the highest in the southeast — meaning VA pays a larger fraction of every MMIS-modular dollar from general fund. This **lowers** state price sensitivity to federally-matched line items (where the 75%/90% match dominates) but **raises** price sensitivity to non-matched components. Price the VA contract accordingly.
14. **(Phase 2) WV is the lowest-onboarding-cost pilot.** ~500K enrollees, single-vendor Gainwell MMIS, 4 MHT MCOs + 1 MHP, voluntary HIE — estimated **~47 person-weeks total lift** (~45% lighter than NC primary buildout). Use WV as the proof-of-cookie-cutter for the Phase 3 (national) rollout playbook.
15. **(Phase 2) State qui tam exposure escalates.** VA's **VFATA** (§ 8.01-216.1) and TN's **Medicaid FCA** (§ 71-5-181) create active state plaintiff-bar pipelines parallel to federal FCA. MedGuard360's audit-service WORM evidence bundle must satisfy both federal Rule 9(b) and state-specific pleading requirements; build a per-state evidence-bundle template variant.
16. **(Phase 2) HIE landscape stratifies.** NC: mandatory-but-suspended (NCGS 90-414.4). VA/WV: voluntary with statutory anchor (W. Va. § 16-29B for WVHIN). TN: voluntary + multi-HIE (TnHIE statewide + OnePartner regional after HIP TN disbandment). The `hie-service` strategy-pattern adapter now needs a **multi-HIE router** specifically for TN — the only pilot requiring simultaneous integration with two HIEs.
17. **(Phase 2) Expansion-FMAP economics split the six.** NC + VA + WV all collect 90% federal on expansion populations. SC, GA, TN have no 90% expansion match (GA's Pathways gets traditional FMAP only). For MedGuard360 enrollment-projection ROI modeling, expansion states front-load federal share recovery from fraud-prevented dollars — particularly NC and VA, where expansion populations are large.
18. **(Phase 3) Medicare MAC inventory expands from 3 to 5.** Phase 3 adds **First Coast JN (FL only)**, **Novitas JH (MS → unlocks AR/CO/LA/NM/OK/TX)**, and **Novitas JL (MD → unlocks DE/DC/NJ/PA)**. Alabama re-uses Palmetto JJ from GA/TN ♻️. Total Medicare A/B MAC inventory across all 10 pilots: **JM (4 states), JJ (3 states), JN (1 state), JH (1 state), JL (1 state)**.
19. **(Phase 3) DME MAC monoculture breaks in Maryland.** All 9 pilots through MS continue using CGS JC. **MD is the first Noridian JA pilot** — entirely new vendor relationship, submitter registration, EDI Gateway, RACI. JA covers the entire Northeast (12 states); the MD buildout is the strategic beachhead for any future Northeast expansion.
20. **(Phase 3) Alabama is the architecturally simplest pilot of all 10.** AL has **zero risk-bearing MCOs** — only 7 PCCM-Entity ACHN regions on a PMPM care-coordination fee on top of FFS. 100% of claim traffic flows through one Gainwell rail. This collapses the MCO connector layer to zero and makes Alabama the **proof-of-cookie-cutter for any future FFS-dominant state** in Phase 3+.
21. **(Phase 3) Mississippi delivers the highest FMAP economics.** At ~77% federal share, MS is the **highest-FMAP state in the nation**. Combined with 75–90% federal match on MMIS-adjacent functions (MITA + SMC), MedGuard360 state cost-share in MS is the lowest of any pilot — making MS the most price-favorable procurement environment.
22. **(Phase 3) Maryland is the strategically highest-leverage Phase-3 pilot despite being the heaviest lift.** A single MD buildout delivers **three beachheads simultaneously**: (a) Novitas JL Medicare connector (DE/DC/MD/NJ/PA), (b) Noridian JA DME connector (entire Northeast), (c) Conduent MMIS pattern ♻️ reusable with VA. MD also has the **only TEFCA QHIN HIE (CRISP)** and the **only state with all-payer hospital rate-setting (HSCRC TCOC)**. MD's HSCRC integration is unique nationally and creates an MD-specific "case-mix gaming under TCOC" fraud class for the fraud-engine.
23. **(Phase 3) BH carve-out taxonomy now has 3 distinct patterns across all 10 pilots.** **(a) NC Tailored Plans** = capitated PIHP carve-out (4 LME/MCOs). **(b) MD PBHS** = ASO-FFS carve-out (Carelon routes claims FFS through Conduent MMIS, NOT capitated). **(c) Everyone else** = integrated within MCO. The `state-config-service` BH-routing dimension now has three values: `pihp_capitated`, `aso_ffs`, or `integrated`.
24. **(Phase 3) Florida's regional matrix is the most complex MCO topology of any pilot.** 11 SMMC regions × ~9 MMA plans = up to ~50 region-plan pairs, plus 3 statewide dental plans and several specialty plans (CMS Children's, SMI, HIV, Child Welfare). State-config-service for FL must support a region × plan matrix that no other pilot requires. FL's APD iConnect (iBudget) for I/DD is also operationally separate from FMMIS — a distinct sub-connector.
25. **(Phase 3) State FCA exposure now spans 5 of 10 pilots.** **Private qui tam**: FL (Ch. 68), MD (§ 2-1601), TN (§ 71-5-181), VA (VFATA § 8.01-216.1). **State-led only**: MS (§ 43-13-225). **No state FCA analog**: NC, SC, GA, AL, WV (federal FCA only). Build per-state evidence-bundle templates accordingly; private-qui-tam states require higher-fidelity Rule-9(b)-equivalent pleading support.
26. **(Phase 3) NEMT routing fragmentation peaks in Maryland.** Most pilots have either statewide brokers (MTM in AL/MS/WV/VA) or per-MCO brokers (NC/SC/FL/TN). **MD uniquely puts NEMT under 24 county health departments** — 24 separate jurisdictional broker contracts. MedGuard360's `nemt-service` needs a per-jurisdiction routing dimension specifically for MD.
27. **(Phase 3) Pharmacy carve patterns continue to diverge.** FL = Magellan FFS + plan PBMs. AL = state-direct AMA Pharmacy Program (most centralized). MS = DOM FFS PBM + MCO PBMs. MD = plan PBMs + state MPP for carve-out drug classes (most fragmented). NCPDP D.0 is universal at the transport layer; the carve dimensions live in `state-config-service`.
28. **(Phase 3) OBBB work-reporting affects only Maryland in this batch.** FL/AL/MS have no expansion population subject to OBBB. MD's CY2027 phase-in requires a work-reporting verification connector — the same connector pattern as NC/VA/WV, build once and gate by state config.
29. **(Phase 3) MMIS vendor mix now stratifies 7-Gainwell : 2-Conduent : 1-multi.** Gainwell: NC, GA, WV, FL, AL, MS, TN (partial). Conduent: VA (legacy) + MD. SC: BCBS-SC heritage `[Confirm]`. TN: multi-vendor. The Gainwell-pattern X12 adapter now serves the majority of all pilots — strongest single-vendor reuse economy in the platform.
